Hardware enablement (HWE) stacks

The HWE stack includes a rolling kernel for the most recent Ubuntu LTS release, so that users can take advantage of new hardware platform support of newer kernels. The final HWE kernel for an LTS release is the same as the next LTS release, and receives the LTS commitment.

Secure data and AI applications with confidential computing based on Intel TDX

Threats to an enterprise's data continue to evolve and become more sophisticated. Notifications of new data breaches are becoming more frequent, and they arise from both internal threats as well as external actors that have managed to compromise the enterprise's infrastructure.

Confidential computing is a technology that protects data when it's in use, even against a zero-day vulnerability in the hypervisor or attackers that have access to the servers - whether that's a rogue administrator, your cloud provider or an external attacker.

Canonical has integrated the Intel TDX technology, and made it available for use in Ubuntu on Intel hardware that supports it. Either deploy confidential computing in a private datacenter, or access it in the leading public clouds, including Azure, Google Cloud, or directly in the Intel Tiber Developer Cloud.
