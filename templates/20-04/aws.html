Standard support for Ubuntu 20.04 LTS ends on May 31, 2025

Transitioning to an Ubuntu version with active standard support (Ubuntu 22.04 LTS or 24.04 LTS) is crucial for performance, hardware compatibility, and access to new technologies, making it ideal for new workloads. However, for existing deployments, the process may be more complex.

Learn more about the Ubuntu lifecycle and release ›

What are your options?

  1. Upgrade to an Ubuntu version with active standard support (Ubuntu 22.04 LTS or 24.04 LTS)

    For Ubuntu 22.04 LTS (support through 2027):

    • Upgrade directly from Ubuntu 20.04 LTS
    • Launch a new instance of Ubuntu 22.04 LTS on AWS

    For Ubuntu 24.04 LTS (support through 2029):

    • Upgrade to Ubuntu 22.04 LTS first and then upgrade to 24.04 LTS
    • Launch a new instance of Ubuntu 24.04 LTS on AWS
    • Contact us to purchase annual subscription tokens

  2. Attach an Ubuntu Pro 20.04 subscription to expand your security maintenance (support through 2030).

    • In-place upgrade from Ubuntu 20.04 to Ubuntu Pro 20.04
    • Fresh installation of Ubuntu Pro 20.04
    • Contact us to purchase annual subscription tokens

    Ubuntu Pro 20.04 LTS expanded security maintenance (ESM) for your entire software supply chain, including:

    • The Linux kernel, Ubuntu base OS
    • Key infrastructure components
    • Over 25,000 open-source applications until 2030, adding 5 years of support.
    • 24-hour critical vulnerability fixes, Landscape, kernel livepatching, hardening scripts based on CIS and DISA-STIG standards, and FIPS 140 compliance, ensuring your infrastructure meets U.S. government security requirements for cryptographic modules

    Ubuntu Pro 20.04 is available with pay-as-you-go metered pricing on AWS, with charges automatically billed to your monthly AWS bill for easy procurement. Additionally, Ubuntu Pro subscriptions can count towards your PPA/EDP spend commitments.
